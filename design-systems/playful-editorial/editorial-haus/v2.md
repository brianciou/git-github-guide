# Design Style: Editorial Haus

## 1. Design Philosophy

**"Build smarter, not just prettier."**

This style takes the clarity and confidence of a beautifully printed independent magazine and layers it with the playfulness of hand-drawn annotations. It rejects the cold minimalism of modern SaaS in favor of editorial gravitas — deep forest greens instead of black, expressive italic serifs instead of neutral sans, and hand-drawn stickers pinned in the margins like a creative director's notes.

### Core DNA

This style embraces:

- **Clean Editorial Canvas**: Near-white backgrounds dominate the page. The visual rhythm comes from occasional blocks of sage green and deep forest, not from busy patterns or textures. Every surface should feel printed, not screened.
- **Italic Emphasis (Latin only)**: The signature move is using italic serif phrases to pull key ideas out of the surrounding text. Italics are not decoration — they are the rhythm of the voice. This rule applies only to Latin text; CJK text uses weight, color, or bracket framing for emphasis instead.
- **Playful Annotation**: Hand-drawn sticker icons (bolts, megaphones, flames, cursor arrows) pin themselves to card corners like margin doodles, puncturing any risk of stiffness.
- **Deep Forest Authority**: A rich forest green does the work that black would normally do, bringing natural warmth and calm confidence to darker emphasis blocks.
- **Capsule Softness**: Every interactive element is a fully-rounded pill, creating a soft counterpoint to the sharp, expressive serif letterforms.

### What this style is NOT

- Rejects pure black (`#000`) — deep forest green carries the dark weight instead
- Rejects sans-serif headlines — all display type is serif, italic serif is the hero move
- Rejects flat corporate illustration and generic stock icons — uses hand-drawn sticker elements instead
- Rejects rigid symmetric grids — embraces asymmetric composition with margin annotations
- Rejects pure geometric rigor — capsule CTAs and rounded card corners soften every hard edge
- Rejects heavy backgrounds — the page is mostly white, with sage and forest as accent blocks only

### Vibe

Like flipping through a beautifully printed independent magazine in a plant-filled studio — bright, airy pages with occasional bands of forest green and sage, and someone has stuck playful hand-drawn annotations in the margins.

## 2. Design Token System

> **Token Override Rule (Critical).** Every token listed in this section is a hard specification, not a recommendation. If you are operating in **Mode A (Full Override)**, you must replace the equivalent token in the target project even if the existing value looks similar. Do not keep the existing background just because both are "warm off-whites" — the whole point of applying this design system is that the specific values here are the correct ones. If you are in **Mode B (Progressive Blend)**, replace every token that is NOT on the user's preservation list, using the same strictness. When in doubt, replace. "Close enough" is never close enough.

### Colors

**Mode:** Light (primary) with Deep Forest accent blocks

- **Background:** `#FAFAF7` (Bone White)
  A near-white with the faintest warm tint — NOT a cream or off-white, but a clean printed-paper white. This is the dominant page surface. If you are tempted to use a warmer or darker value because it "looks similar enough," stop — that instinct is exactly the failure mode this rule prevents.

- **Foreground:** `#1F3A2E` (Deep Forest)
  The signature dark color. A rich, dark forest green that does the work of black without its coldness. Used for all primary text, deep emphasis blocks, and structural borders. The green undertone keeps the whole system feeling organic and warm.

- **Surface Light:** `#FFFFFF` (Paper White)
  Pure white used inside cards and nested containers to lift them off the bone-white background. Slightly brighter than the main background — this is intentional.

- **Accent:** `#B8D9A8` (Sage Mint)
  A soft, dusty green used as a pill button background, a highlight fill, and most importantly as an alternate block background to break up the white. This is the secondary surface color, not a tiny highlight.

- **Accent Ink:** `#1F3A2E` (Deep Forest on Sage)
  Text placed on Sage Mint surfaces uses Deep Forest, never black, to maintain tonal harmony.

- **Muted:** `#E8E3D8` (Aged Cream)
  A soft warm-neutral used for pill tag backgrounds and subtle divider fills. Used sparingly — not as a primary surface.

- **Muted Foreground:** `#5A6B5C` (Sage Grey)
  A green-leaning grey for metadata, captions, and secondary body text. Never a neutral grey — always tinted toward the forest palette.

- **Border:** `#1F3A2E` (Deep Forest)
  All structural borders use the foreground color at full strength. Borders are assertive in this system, not subtle.

**Color Proportion Target (Critical):**

- **Bone White (`#FAFAF7`):** ~80% of page surface. This is the basic page canvas. Most sections, most cards, most regions sit on Bone White.
- **Sage Mint (`#B8D9A8`):** ~15% of page surface. Used for complete background blocks (whole hero sections, highlight cards, callout panels) to break up the white and create rhythm — NOT as a small accent trim.
- **Deep Forest (`#1F3A2E`):** ~5% of page surface. Reserved for highest-impact emphasis — one full-bleed dark section per page maximum, plus text, borders, and pill buttons.

Always err toward more white and fewer tinted blocks. A page that uses these colors but in the wrong ratio is not implementing this design system correctly.

### Typography

**Font Stack:**

- **Display (Headlines & Italic Emphasis):** `'DM Serif Display', 'Noto Serif TC', Georgia, serif`
  A high-contrast transitional serif with a beautifully expressive italic variant. The italic is the whole point — it should feel like a calligraphic gesture, not just slanted roman text. Noto Serif TC is chained after so that Chinese characters fall through to a proper CJK serif when present.

- **Body:** `'Inter', 'Noto Sans TC', system-ui, sans-serif`
  A clean, highly readable sans-serif that gets out of the way and lets the serif headlines perform. Noto Sans TC is chained for CJK fallback.

- **Label / Metadata:** `'Inter', 'Noto Sans TC', system-ui, sans-serif`
  Uppercase, tracked, used inside bracket tags and pill labels.

**Type Scale (Mobile-First — MUST use responsive prefixes):**

- **H1 (Hero Headlines):** `text-4xl md:text-6xl lg:text-8xl`
  Display serif, `leading-[0.95]` (very tight line-height), `tracking-tight` (slightly tight letter-spacing). Mix roman and italic within the same headline — italic phrases pull focus like pull quotes. **Mobile size: 36px. Tablet: 60px. Desktop: 96px.** Never skip the mobile base size.

- **H2 (Section Headers):** `text-3xl md:text-4xl lg:text-5xl`
  Display serif, often italic for sentiment, roman for statements. `leading-tight`. Mobile 30px → Desktop 48px.

- **H3 (Card Titles):** `text-xl md:text-2xl lg:text-3xl`
  Display serif, weight 400. Mobile 20px → Desktop 30px.

- **Body Text:** `text-base md:text-lg`
  Inter, `leading-relaxed` (line-height 1.625) for Latin, `leading-[1.8]` for CJK content. Color Deep Forest or Sage Grey for secondary text. **Minimum mobile body text size: 16px. Never shrink below `text-base` on mobile for body content.**

- **Metadata / Labels:** `text-xs` (12px fixed)
  Inter uppercase, `tracking-[0.2em]` (wide letter spacing 0.2em), wrapped in bracket markers like `[ THE STRATEGY HAUS ]`. Only metadata/labels can use 12px — body text never.

**Text Transform Conventions:**
- Uppercase for: brand tags (always in brackets), pill labels, small captions, category markers
- Sentence case for: all headlines, body text, button labels
- Italic for: the emotional or emphatic half of Latin headlines. Never italicize a whole headline — always mix roman and italic. **Never apply italic to CJK characters** (see CJK Adaptation in Section 10).

### Radius & Border

**Radius System (Deliberately Mixed):**

- **Interactive elements (buttons, pills, tags):** `rounded-full` — fully rounded capsule shape
- **Containers (cards, sections, inputs):** `rounded-3xl` (1.5rem / 24px) — generously rounded rectangles
- **Small decorative chips:** `rounded-full`

The contrast is intentional: pills signal "click me", large-radius rectangles signal "content lives here". Never use sharp corners (`rounded-none`).

**Border Width:**
- Standard: `border` (1px solid) in Deep Forest
- Button outlines: `border` (1px solid)
- Emphasis only: `border-2` (2px solid) — used only for featured or selected states

**Border Style:** Always solid. Never dashed, dotted, or double.

### Shadows & Effects

**Philosophy:** Flat. No drop shadows, no glows, no depth effects. Separation is achieved through color blocking and border contrast.

**No effects:**
- No drop shadows of any kind
- No blur or glassmorphism
- No gradients or mesh backgrounds
- No inner shadows or glow effects

### Textures & Patterns

Clean flat surfaces. Visual interest comes from hand-drawn sticker decorations (see Bold Factor), not background textures.

### Extended Palette (Data & Content Colors)

The Core Palette above handles UI chrome. When content needs multiple distinguishable colors — step diagrams, flow charts, category labels, comparison cards, directional arrows, chart series — use ONLY these Extended Palette colors. Every color is a tonal variation of the Core Palette's green/cream family.

> **The cardinal rule**: Never introduce colors from outside this Extended Palette. No Tailwind default blues, oranges, purples, or reds. No legacy project colors. If you need a color for a diagram arrow, step indicator, or category label, it comes from this table or it doesn't exist.

| Token | Value | Derivation | Usage |
|:------|:------|:-----------|:------|
| `extended-1` | `#D4E8C7` | Sage Mint lightened 30% | Step 1, primary category, lightest fill for step cards |
| `extended-2` | `#8FB578` | Sage Mint darkened 20% | Step 2, secondary category, stronger emphasis |
| `extended-3` | `#E8E3D8` | Muted (Aged Cream) | Step 3, neutral/default category, subtle fills |
| `extended-4` | `#4A6B54` | Midpoint between Deep Forest and Sage Mint | Step 4, diagram connectors, arrow color A |
| `extended-5` | `#2D4F3A` | Deep Forest lightened 10% | Step 5, secondary connector, arrow color B |
| `extended-6` | `#C5B99B` | Warm neutral | Optional: callout borders, quote backgrounds |

**Arrow and connector colors**: Directional arrows in flow diagrams must use `extended-4` (#4A6B54), `extended-5` (#2D4F3A), or `#1F3A2E` (Deep Forest). Never use red, blue, purple, or any non-green hue for arrows.

**Step indicator colors**: For numbered step sequences, cycle through `extended-1` → `extended-2` → `extended-3` → `extended-4`. If more than 4 steps, repeat the cycle or use opacity variations.

## 3. Component Stylings

### Buttons

**Primary Button (Sage Pill):**
```tsx
className="inline-flex items-center gap-2 rounded-full bg-[#B8D9A8] px-6 py-3 text-sm font-medium text-[#1F3A2E] transition-colors duration-200 hover:bg-[#8FB578] min-h-[44px]"
```
- **Shape:** Fully rounded pill (`rounded-full` = border-radius: 9999px)
- **Colors:** Sage Mint background, Deep Forest text
- **Padding:** `px-6 py-3` = 24px horizontal, 12px vertical
- **Hover:** background darkens to `extended-2` — no lift, no shadow, just color shift
- **Signature detail:** Always prefixed with a small filled dot (●) or icon to the left of the label
- **Touch target:** `min-h-[44px]` ensures minimum 44px tap height for accessibility

**Secondary Button (Outline Pill):**
```tsx
className="inline-flex items-center gap-2 rounded-full border border-[#1F3A2E] bg-transparent px-6 py-3 text-sm font-medium text-[#1F3A2E] transition-colors duration-200 hover:bg-[#1F3A2E] hover:text-[#FAFAF7] min-h-[44px]"
```
- **Shape:** Same pill as primary
- **Colors:** Transparent background, Deep Forest border (`border` = 1px solid) and text
- **Hover:** fills with Deep Forest, text becomes Bone White (color inversion)
- **Use when:** secondary or tertiary actions

**Arrow Pill Button (Icon-Only):**
```tsx
<button
  aria-label="Continue"
  className="inline-flex items-center justify-center rounded-full border border-[#1F3A2E] bg-transparent w-14 h-11 text-[#1F3A2E] transition-colors duration-200 hover:bg-[#1F3A2E] hover:text-[#FAFAF7]"
>
  →
</button>
```
- **Shape:** Wide short pill roughly 56×44px (`w-14 h-11`)
- **Icon-only:** requires `aria-label` for screen readers
- **Signature element:** this is a recognizable Editorial Haus component — a "continue reading" button usually positioned bottom-left of cards

**Ghost Text Link:**
```tsx
className="text-[#1F3A2E] underline-offset-4 decoration-1 hover:underline font-serif italic"
```
- **Text-only link** with italic serif styling
- **Underline appears on hover** with 4px offset from baseline
- **Voice match:** italic serif matches the editorial voice of the system (Latin text only — for CJK, use weight or color)

### Cards / Containers

**Standard Card (White on Bone White):**
```tsx
<div className="rounded-3xl bg-white p-6 md:p-8 lg:p-10">
```
- **Background:** Pure white, lifts slightly off the Bone White page
- **Radius:** `rounded-3xl` (24px) — generously rounded, never square
- **Padding:** scales mobile → desktop (`p-6` = 24px mobile, `p-10` = 40px desktop)
- **No border** by default — contrast with the page comes from the tiny brightness difference

**Dark Editorial Card (Deep Forest):**
```tsx
<div className="rounded-3xl bg-[#1F3A2E] p-6 md:p-8 lg:p-10 text-[#FAFAF7]">
```
- **Background:** Deep Forest green
- **Text color:** Bone White (never pure white, never gray)
- **Use:** one per page for highest-impact emphasis — do not overuse
- **Note:** This is where the ~5% Deep Forest proportion lives

**Sage Highlight Card (Sage Mint):**
```tsx
<div className="rounded-3xl bg-[#B8D9A8] p-6 md:p-8 lg:p-10 text-[#1F3A2E]">
```
- **Background:** Sage Mint
- **Text color:** Deep Forest (never black)
- **Use:** highlight callouts, featured content, alternate block backgrounds
- **Note:** This is where most of the ~15% Sage Mint proportion lives

**Cards with Corner Stickers (Signature Pattern):**
```tsx
<div className="relative rounded-3xl bg-[#B8D9A8] p-8 lg:p-10">
  <span className="absolute -top-3 -left-3 text-3xl rotate-[-5deg]" aria-hidden="true">⚡</span>
  <span className="absolute -bottom-3 -right-3 text-3xl rotate-[3deg]" aria-hidden="true">🔥</span>
  {/* card content */}
</div>
```
- **Relative positioning** on the card enables absolute sticker children
- **Stickers bleed outside** the card edge with negative offsets (`-top-3 -left-3`)
- **Slight rotation** (`rotate-[-5deg]`, `rotate-[3deg]`) mimics hand-pinned feel
- **Decorative:** `aria-hidden="true"` so screen readers skip them
- **Use emoji or inline SVG** — do not use clean thin-stroke icons here

### Inputs

**Text Input (Pill-Shaped):**
```tsx
className="w-full rounded-full border border-[#1F3A2E] bg-transparent px-6 py-3 text-base text-[#1F3A2E] placeholder:text-[#5A6B5C] focus-visible:outline-none focus-visible:ring-2 focus-visible:ring-[#1F3A2E] focus-visible:ring-offset-2 focus-visible:ring-offset-[#FAFAF7] min-h-[44px]"
```
- **Shape:** Fully rounded pill matching button family
- **Border:** 1px Deep Forest, transparent fill
- **Placeholder text:** Sage Grey
- **Focus state:** 2px Deep Forest ring with 2px offset against Bone White — keyboard-visible only (`focus-visible` prevents it from showing on mouse click)
- **Touch target:** `min-h-[44px]`

**Pill Tag (Selectable Chip):**
```tsx
<span className="inline-flex items-center rounded-full border border-[#1F3A2E] bg-white px-4 py-1.5 text-xs font-medium uppercase tracking-[0.15em] text-[#1F3A2E]">
  LAYOUT STRATEGY
</span>
```
- **Used for:** category tags, topic markers inside cards
- **Styling:** uppercase, `tracking-[0.15em]` (wide letter spacing 0.15em)
- **Small:** `text-xs` (12px) — this is the one context where 12px is acceptable
- **Shape:** same pill family

### Icons

**Two parallel icon systems:**

**1. UI icons (`lucide-react`):**
- Used for: arrows, dots, close buttons, navigation chevrons
- Stroke width: `stroke-1.5`
- Color: Deep Forest
- Clean and minimal — these sit inside buttons and navigation

**2. Decorative stickers (emoji/SVG):**
- Used for: corner decorations on feature cards, margin annotations
- Default approach: large Unicode/emoji characters (⚡🔥📣🖱️✽) at `text-2xl` to `text-3xl`
- Always apply a slight rotation transform (`rotate-[-5deg]` or `rotate-[3deg]`) to mimic hand-pinned feel
- **Do not substitute with clean thin-stroke lucide icons** — the hand-drawn irregularity is essential to the style

**Asterisk Flourish:**
Every page features ✽ as a brand flourish, typically top-right of the header. Render in Deep Forest at `text-3xl` to `text-5xl` using the serif font. Mark it `aria-hidden="true"` since it's decorative.

## 4. Non-Genericness (The "Bold Factor")

These are the **mandatory bold choices** that prevent generic output and define this style's personality. Every implementation must include these moves.

> **Applying Bold Factor — two rules you must follow.**
>
> **Rule 1: Attempt the main move first.** If a bold move references a specific asset or technique you don't have, do NOT immediately fall back to a generic substitute. First try to produce the main move using inline SVG, CSS shapes, or Unicode characters. Only if that fails should you consider a fallback — and the fallback must still carry the same visual weight as the original.
>
> **Rule 2: When a bold move doesn't apply to the current content, substitute — do not skip.** Find a contextually appropriate substitute that carries the same signature energy.

### Mandatory Bold Choices

**1. Italic Serif Emphasis Phrases (Latin only)**

Every major Latin headline must mix roman and italic serif. The italic portion carries the emotional weight.

```tsx
<h1 className="font-serif text-4xl md:text-6xl lg:text-8xl leading-[0.95] text-[#1F3A2E]">
  Your dream brand deserves a strategy that works.{" "}
  <em className="italic">Let's build it together.</em>
</h1>
```

- Never set an entire Latin headline in all-italic or all-roman
- **For CJK text:** do NOT apply italic — it synthesizes an uncomfortable slant. Use weight shift (`font-weight: 500`), color shift, or bracket framing (`「」` / `《》`) instead. See Section 10 CJK Adaptation.
- **For mixed Latin-CJK headlines:** italicize only the Latin portion, leave CJK upright:
  ```tsx
  <h1>Your brand <em className="italic">deserves better</em> — 你的品牌值得更好</h1>
  ```

**2. Hand-Drawn Corner Stickers**

Cards should have sticker decorations pinned to their corners, bleeding slightly outside the card edge with negative position offsets. Use Unicode emoji or inline SVG as the default — do not skip stickers because custom SVG assets are unavailable. Emoji + rotation is the default approach, not a fallback.

**3. Bracket Brand Tags**

The brand or section name always appears wrapped in square brackets: `[ THE STRATEGY HAUS ]`. Use in headers, footers, and section markers. Styled as `text-xs uppercase tracking-[0.2em] font-medium`.

**4. Asterisk Flourish**

A six-pointed asterisk (✽) appears on every page as a brand flourish. Typically top-right of the header, sometimes center-top of a section. Size `text-3xl` to `text-5xl`.

**5. Inline Decorative Element in Headlines**

Insert a small decorative element inline within hero headlines — a small oval image pill, a decorative Unicode character (◆ ✦ ☘) inside a colored pill span, or a sticker emoji that interrupts the text flow. The point is that headlines should have visual interruptions, not just text.

**6. Dual-Toned Section Blocks (with proportion discipline)**

Pages alternate between Bone White (dominant), Sage Mint (secondary), and Deep Forest (rare emphasis). Every long page MUST include at least one full-bleed Deep Forest section for visual contrast. Sage Mint appears as 1-2 full-block sections for rhythm. White dominates — do not flip the proportions.

**7. Pill CTAs With Leading Dot**

Every primary CTA button has a small filled circle (●) to the left of the label text:

```tsx
<button className="inline-flex items-center gap-2 rounded-full bg-[#B8D9A8] px-6 py-3 ...">
  <span className="w-1.5 h-1.5 rounded-full bg-current" />
  JOIN US TODAY
</button>
```

**8. Mixed Radius System (Pills + Rounded Rectangles)**

Interactive elements are `rounded-full` (fully rounded capsules); containers are `rounded-3xl` (24px rounded rectangles). A button with `rounded-xl` or a card with `rounded-full` both break the system.

## 5. Layout Strategy

### Container

**Max Width:** `max-w-6xl` (1152px)
Centered with `mx-auto`, horizontal padding `px-4 md:px-6 lg:px-8` (mobile-first).

### Grid System

**Base:** 12-column grid used asymmetrically. Prefer 7/5, 8/4, or stacked-with-offset layouts over 6/6 symmetric splits. On mobile, all multi-column grids collapse to single column.

### Spacing

**Philosophy:** Generous breathing room. Editorial pacing, not dashboard density.

- Section padding (mobile-first): `py-12 md:py-20 lg:py-28`
- Card padding (mobile-first): `p-6 md:p-8 lg:p-10`
- Gap between cards (mobile-first): `gap-4 md:gap-6 lg:gap-8`

### Z-Index Layers

- Header (sticky): `z-40`
- Sticker decorations: `z-10`
- Overlays / modals: `z-50`

## 6. Effects & Animation

### Motion Philosophy

**Soft and considered.** Nothing snaps; nothing bounces. Transitions feel like pages turning in a well-bound book.

**Transition Defaults:**
```tsx
"transition-colors duration-200 ease-out"      /* color-only changes */
"transition-all duration-300 ease-out"         /* multi-property hover states */
```

### Hover Behaviors

1. **Color fill:** Buttons fill with inverted color scheme on hover. No translate, no shadow.
2. **Background tint:** Content cards gain subtle background shift.
3. **Arrow translate:** Arrow icon inside arrow pill button translates `2px` right on hover.
4. **Sticker wiggle (optional):** Corner stickers rotate `±2deg` on card hover.

### Micro-interactions

- Input focus: ring fades in over 150ms
- Accordion expand: smooth grid-row transition over 300ms, no bounce
- No parallax, no scroll-triggered animations

## 7. Spacing, Layout & Iconography

### Default Max-Width

`max-w-6xl` (1152px).

### Spacing System

8px base grid. All spacing values are multiples of 8 to ensure precise pixel alignment.

- Tight: `gap-2` (8px), `p-2`
- Standard: `gap-4` (16px), `p-4`
- Comfortable: `gap-8` (32px), `p-8`
- Spacious: `gap-16` (64px), `py-16`

**Mobile-first scaling:** Start with the smaller value, add `md:` / `lg:` prefixes to grow. Never write `p-10` without a smaller mobile fallback.

### Iconography Integration

- **UI icons (lucide-react):** Inside buttons, navigation markers, leading-dot on CTAs. Deep Forest, `stroke-1.5`.
- **Decorative stickers:** Pinned to card corners. Never inside buttons or navigation.
- **Asterisk flourish:** One per page, header region.

## 8. Responsive Strategy

> **Responsive Mandate (Critical).** Every visual specification in this document applies to both desktop and mobile. Responsive design is not an optional polish step — it is a hard requirement. A page that looks correct at 1440px but clips, overflows, or breaks at 375px is not finished. If you are thinking "I'll handle mobile later", stop — handle it now, in the same edit, using the rules below. Before declaring any page complete, you MUST mentally (or actually) resize the viewport to 375px width and verify: no horizontal scroll, no clipped text, no overflowing containers, no unreadable font sizes. If any of these fail, the implementation has failed.

### Breakpoints

Mobile-first. Define base styles for mobile, then layer tablet and desktop refinements.

- **Mobile:** `< 768px` (default, no prefix)
- **Tablet:** `md:` (768px and up)
- **Desktop:** `lg:` (1024px and up)

### Mobile Adaptations

1. **Grid Collapse.** Multi-column grids → `grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 lg:grid-cols-3`. Never skip the mobile default.

2. **Typography Scaling (mobile-first).** Headlines scale UP with breakpoint prefixes, not down:
   - H1 hero: `text-4xl md:text-6xl lg:text-8xl` (36px → 60px → 96px)
   - H2 section: `text-3xl md:text-4xl lg:text-5xl` (30px → 36px → 48px)
   - H3 card: `text-xl md:text-2xl lg:text-3xl` (20px → 24px → 30px)
   - Body: `text-base md:text-lg` (16px → 18px)

   Italic emphasis (Latin only) is preserved at every size. CJK headlines never drop below `text-3xl` (30px).

3. **Padding Reduction (mobile-first).** Section padding scales up:
   ```
   py-12 md:py-20 lg:py-28
   px-4 md:px-6 lg:px-8
   ```
   Card padding scales similarly:
   ```
   p-6 md:p-8 lg:p-10
   ```

4. **Sticker Scaling.** Corner stickers shrink from `text-3xl` on desktop to `text-2xl` on mobile. Position offsets reduce from `-top-3` to `-top-2`. Never remove stickers entirely — they are signature.

5. **Touch Targets.** All interactive elements `min-h-[44px] min-w-[44px]`. Pill buttons at default sizing usually satisfy this; verify anyway.

6. **CTA Buttons.** Full width on mobile: `w-full md:w-auto`. A full-width pill is still a pill.

7. **Navigation.** Desktop nav links hide on mobile (`hidden md:flex`); replace with a hamburger menu button inside a 44px touch-target container (matching the arrow pill button pattern).

8. **Horizontal Overflow Prevention.** Check every horizontal element for mobile overflow:
   - Tab/pill groups (like the `Part 1 / Part 2 / 推薦資源` tabs): wrap in `<div className="overflow-x-auto -mx-4 px-4">` so they scroll edge-to-edge when they exceed viewport width
   - Tables: wrap in `<div className="overflow-x-auto">` OR switch to stacked label-value pairs on mobile
   - Code blocks: use `overflow-x-auto` on `<pre>` with `text-xs md:text-sm`
   - SVG diagrams: set `max-w-full h-auto`, use `viewBox` instead of fixed width/height
   - Images: always add `max-w-full h-auto`

9. **CJK Text on Mobile.** Do NOT shrink body text below `text-base` (16px) when content contains Chinese characters — CJK characters become illegible at smaller sizes. Hero CJK headlines never drop below `text-3xl` (30px). Apply `leading-[1.8]` or `leading-loose` for CJK body paragraphs — standard Latin leading is too tight for CJK.

### Overflow Check — mandatory before completion

Before declaring the page done, verify at 375px viewport width:

- [ ] No horizontal scrollbar on `<body>`
- [ ] Hero headline fits without clipping (no characters cut off at edge)
- [ ] Tab/pill groups either fit or scroll cleanly
- [ ] All SVG diagrams and images scale to viewport width
- [ ] Code blocks have horizontal scroll or reduced font size
- [ ] Minimum font size anywhere is 14px (12px only for metadata/labels)
- [ ] All touch targets meet 44×44px

If any item fails, the responsive implementation is incomplete. Fix before moving on.

## 9. Accessibility & Best Practices

### Contrast Ratios

- **Deep Forest (`#1F3A2E`) on Bone White (`#FAFAF7`):** ~11:1 — **AAA compliant**
- **Deep Forest (`#1F3A2E`) on Sage Mint (`#B8D9A8`):** ~7.5:1 — **AAA compliant**
- **Bone White (`#FAFAF7`) on Deep Forest (`#1F3A2E`):** ~11:1 — **AAA compliant**
- **Sage Grey (`#5A6B5C`) on Bone White:** ~4.8:1 — **AA compliant** (metadata only)

**Avoid:** Sage Mint text on Bone White. Sage Grey on Sage Mint. Pure black or pure white anywhere except where specified.

### Focus States

```tsx
"focus-visible:outline-none focus-visible:ring-2 focus-visible:ring-[#1F3A2E] focus-visible:ring-offset-2 focus-visible:ring-offset-[#FAFAF7]"
```

`:focus-visible` ensures the ring only appears for keyboard navigation, not mouse clicks.

### Semantic HTML

- Use `<header>`, `<nav>`, `<main>`, `<section>`, `<article>`, `<aside>`, `<footer>`

- Use `<button>` for actions, `<a href>` for links
- Use `<em>` for italic emphasis phrases — semantically correct AND visually required
- Form inputs must have `<label>`

- Icon-only buttons: `aria-label` required
- Decorative stickers: `aria-hidden="true"`
- Asterisk flourish: `aria-hidden="true"`
- Accordions: `<button>` with `aria-expanded`
- All interactive elements keyboard accessible

- Inline asterisk flourish as SVG
- Optimize sticker SVGs under 2KB

### Sticker Asset Defaults

Minimum sticker vocabulary: lightning bolt, megaphone, flame, cursor arrow, six-pointed asterisk. Default implementation uses Unicode/emoji characters (⚡📣🔥🖱️✽) rendered at `text-2xl` to `text-3xl` with slight rotation transforms (`rotate-[-5deg]`, `rotate-[3deg]`). This is the default approach — not a fallback — so do not skip stickers because custom SVG assets are unavailable.

### CJK Typography Adaptation

This design system is intended to work with Chinese (and other CJK) content. When CJK characters are present, follow these additional rules:

**Font pairing:** CJK fonts are already chained in the font-family above. Latin characters render in DM Serif Display / Inter; CJK characters automatically fall through to Noto Serif TC / Noto Sans TC.

**Italic handling — critical rule:** Most CJK fonts do NOT have a true italic variant. Browsers synthesize a slanted version via `font-style: italic`, but the result is visually uncomfortable to read. **Do not apply italic to CJK characters, ever — even if the design calls for italic emphasis in headlines.**

Correct approaches:

- **Mixed Latin-CJK headlines with italic emphasis:** Wrap ONLY the Latin portion in `<em>`, leave CJK upright:
  ```tsx
  <h1 className="font-serif text-4xl md:text-6xl">
    Your brand <em className="italic">deserves better</em> — 你的品牌值得更好
  </h1>
  ```
- **Pure CJK headlines where design calls for italic:** Substitute a different emphasis technique:
  - **Weight shift:** `font-weight: 500` or `600` on the emphasized phrase
  - **Color shift:** use accent or darker shade on the emphasized phrase
  - **Bracket framing:** wrap in `「 」` or `《 》` (traditional CJK emphasis method)
  - **Size bump:** make the emphasized phrase slightly larger

- **Never:** Apply italic className to an element containing CJK and expect the browser to handle it. The synthesized slant is visually jarring.

**Line height:** CJK body text needs more generous leading than Latin. Use `leading-[1.8]` or `leading-loose` (line-height: 2) for CJK body paragraphs.

**Letter spacing:** Do NOT apply tracking to CJK body text — CJK characters are already visually spaced by their square em-boxes. Tracking makes them feel loose and unreadable. Tracking IS appropriate for CJK labels in uppercase-equivalent contexts (e.g. `[ PART 1 ]`) — use `tracking-[0.1em]` at those moments.

**Punctuation:** Use `text-align: left` or `text-align: start` for CJK body paragraphs. Justified text creates rivers and uneven gaps in CJK layouts.

**Mixed headlines sizing:** CJK characters often appear visually larger than Latin at the same `font-size` value. If the mix looks unbalanced, scale Latin words up slightly (`<span className="text-[1.1em]">`) or CJK down. Test visually — no universal ratio exists.
